Case Study: Clark County School District

Cyber Attacks on CCSD Nevada's Clark County School District (CCSD) is the nation's fifth largest district, with 304,276 students enrolled in the 2022-2023 reporting year. This represents a significant portion of Nevada's K-12 student population -- approximately 73%!

Having attended all of my K-12 years at Walter V. Long Elementary School, John C. Fremont Middle School, and Las Vegas High School, CCSD is "my district."

Between the years of 2016-2023, CCSD experienced at least six separate data breaches.

A major incident occurred in October of 2023, leading to the compromise of highly sensitive information belonging to the district’s teachers, students, and graduates (and their families)! It is estimated that more than 300K students had their information released, including student pictures, household contact information, medical information, and behavior incident reports.

With approximately 73% of Nevada's K-12 students being served by CCSD, this is a huge portion of the state's overall percentage of students affected by a data breach (79.4%). Put another way, 4 out of every 5 students in Nevada have had their data leaked since 2016.

The leakage of this data through cyberattacks is not only invasive, but it is also frightening. There are broad implications that breaches like this can have on personal privacy, safety, and psychological well-being. In addition, trust can be lost and the educational environment can suffer.

It is therefore essential that CCSD works to ensure robust cybersecurity measures to protect the vulnerable population of students and to maintain the integrity of its institution.
